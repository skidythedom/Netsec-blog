BGP Security and Route Hijacking Prevention

Marcus Lee
5 min read

Introduction

The Border Gateway Protocol powers global internet routing. Despite its importance, BGP was designed without strong built-in authentication mechanisms.

This creates opportunities for route hijacking attacks capable of redirecting internet traffic through malicious networks.

What is BGP?

BGP enables autonomous systems to exchange routing information and determine the best available paths across the internet.

Route Hijacking

Route hijacking occurs when unauthorized systems advertise IP prefixes they do not control.

Such attacks can result in surveillance, denial of service, or traffic manipulation.

Protection Methods

Modern protections include RPKI validation, route filtering, prefix limits, and real-time anomaly monitoring.

Conclusion

As internet infrastructure evolves, securing routing protocols remains essential for maintaining trust and stability.
